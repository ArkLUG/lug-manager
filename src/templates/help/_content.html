Interactive Tour

Getting Started (All Members)
1
Edit your profile — Click the yellow "Edit My Profile" button on the dashboard or members page. Add your name, contact info, and choose your privacy settings for each field.
2
Subscribe to the calendar — On the dashboard, expand the calendar card and copy the iCal URL into your calendar app (Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, Outlook, etc.).
3
Browse members — Go to the Members page to see who's in the LUG. Click "View" on any member to see their shared contact info.
4
Privacy settings — You control who sees your contact info. Set each field (email, phone, address, birthday, Discord) to "Don't share", "Verified members only", or "All members". Verified = has attended in person or paid dues.

Event Manager Guide
1
Create meetings — Go to Meetings and click "New Meeting". Fill in the title, location, date/time, and select your chapter. Virtual meetings hide the location and show a Discord voice channel picker instead.
2
Create events — Go to Events and click "New Event". Events are date-based (no times). Choose scope: Chapter, LUG Wide, or Non-LUG. Non-LUG events don't have status tracking.
3
Take attendance — Click "View" on a meeting or event to open the detail modal. The attendance panel at the bottom lets you search and add members. Use the multi-select dropdown to check in multiple people at once.
4
QR code check-in — In the detail modal, click "Show QR Code" to generate a scannable check-in link. Attendees scan it on their phone and can check in via Discord, by finding their name, or by entering their info as a new member.
5
Publish reports — After a meeting or event, click "Publish Report" to post an attendance summary to the Discord forum channel.

Note: You can only manage meetings/events for chapters where you have the event_manager or lead role.

Chapter Lead Guide
1
Manage members — You can add new members, edit existing ones, and manage dues (mark paid/unpaid). Go to the Members page — you'll see Edit, Dues, and Add Member buttons.
2
View all PII — As a chapter lead, you can see all member contact info regardless of their privacy settings. This is for chapter management purposes.
3
Everything event managers can do — You also have all event manager capabilities: create/edit meetings and events, take attendance, generate QR codes, and publish reports.

Note: Only admins can change member roles, delete members, or edit chapter settings.

{{/is_chapter_lead_role}} {{#is_admin}}
Admin Guide
1
Configure Discord — Go to Settings to set your Guild ID, announcement channels, forum channels, and timezone. The bot needs to be invited to your server with proper permissions first.
2
Map Discord roles — Go to Settings > Role Mappings to map Discord roles to admin or member LUG roles. Members are auto-provisioned when they log in based on these mappings.
3
Set up chapters — Go to Chapters to create sub-groups. Assign leads (who get synced Discord roles) and event managers. Each chapter gets its own announcement channel.
4
Configure perk levels — Go to Perk Levels to define attendance tiers (e.g. Bronze, Silver, Gold) with meeting/event requirements, dues prerequisites, and Discord role rewards.
5
Monitor activity — Use the Attendance Overview to see who's attending, and the Audit Log to track all actions taken by admins and managers.
6
Sync members — In Settings, use "Sync Now" to pull all Discord guild members into the system. This runs automatically every 6 hours. Use "Sync All to Discord" to push meetings/events to Discord.
